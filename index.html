Overview

Accurate 3D scene perception plays an important role for robotic manipulation in unstructured environments. 3D Gaussian Splatting offers real-time and high-quality visual reconstructions with differentiable properties â€“ making it a promising tool not only for visual environment representation but also for physics-aware environment modeling. This has the potential to solve open problems such as state estimation of deformable and articulated objects, world model representation from visual data and efficient sim-to-real transfer. However, leveraging 3D Gaussian Splatting for robotic manipulation presents several open challenges such as representation efficiency, requirements of multi-camera settings, and integration of visual representations with physical parameters. This workshop aims to bring together junior and senior researchers from the robot learning, computer vision and graphics communities to explore the emerging role of 3D Gaussian Splatting for learning robotic manipulation policies from visual data. Although 3D Gaussian Splatting has been mainly deployed for computer graphics tasks, its application in robotics is promising, but necessitates interdisciplinary knowledge. Thus, the primary goal of this workshop is to connect researchers with diverse backgrounds to foster collaboration from heterogeneous communities, a key requirement for exploring novel 3D Gaussian Splatting approaches for robot learning. The program will feature invited talks, poster sessions, and spotlight presentations of selected papers, offering a platform to highlight recent advances and encourage discussion on future research directions.
In the workshop, we will focus on the following research topics:

Representation Efficiency
  • How can 3D Gaussian Splatting efficiently represent highly dynamic and unstructured environments?
  • What methods can improve efficiency and scene representation to enable real-time applications on limited hardware?
Physics-aware World Modeling from Visual Inputs
  • What approaches allow for seamless integration between 3D Gaussian Splatting and physics-based representations to learn robotic manipulation tasks?
  • How can we leverage the differentiable properties of 3D Gaussian Splatting for physical parameters estimation from visual data?
Photorealistic Environment Simulation and Sim-to-Real Policy Transfer
  • How can photorealistic simulations based on 3D Gaussian Splatting help close the sim-to-real gap in robotic manipulation learning?
  • What are the primary challenges and limitations in zero-shot sim-to-real transfer using 3D Gaussian Splatting?
Generalization and Robustness of Representations and Policies
  • What strategies can enhance generalization and robustness of manipulation policies built upon 3D Gaussian Splatting-based inputs?
  • How to improve 3D Gaussian Splatting representations to generalize across diverse tasks and environmental conditions?
Data Augmentation and Environment Manipulation
  • What opportunities does 3D Gaussian Splatting offer for controllable scene editing (e.g., object pose and shape) to enhance downstream policy training?
  • What are the challenges in using 3D Gaussian Splatting as a robust and scalable data augmentation tool?
